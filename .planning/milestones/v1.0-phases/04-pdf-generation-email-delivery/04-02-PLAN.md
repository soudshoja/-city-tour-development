  <name>Task 1: Dispatch SendInvoiceEmailsJob from CreateBulkInvoicesJob</name>
  <files>
    app/Jobs/CreateBulkInvoicesJob.php
  </files>
  <action>
    Modify `app/Jobs/CreateBulkInvoicesJob.php` to dispatch `SendInvoiceEmailsJob` AFTER the transaction completes successfully.

    **CRITICAL: Dispatch OUTSIDE the DB::transaction closure, not inside it.** PDF generation is slow and must not hold database locks. The research explicitly warns against this (Pitfall 1).

    In the `handle()` method, AFTER the `DB::transaction(function () { ... });` call (line ~178), add:

    ```php
    // Dispatch email job AFTER transaction commits successfully
    // PDF generation is CPU-intensive and must not hold DB locks
    SendInvoiceEmailsJob::dispatch($this->bulkUploadId)
        ->onQueue('emails')
        ->afterCommit();

    Log::info('Dispatched email notification job', [
        'bulk_upload_id' => $this->bulkUploadId,
    ]);
    ```

    Add import at top of file:
    ```php
    use App\Jobs\SendInvoiceEmailsJob;
    ```

    Do NOT modify any other part of CreateBulkInvoicesJob. The transaction logic, invoice creation, and failed() handler remain untouched.

    The `afterCommit()` ensures the email job only runs after the 'completed' status is committed to the database (same pattern as BulkInvoiceController::approve dispatching CreateBulkInvoicesJob).

    The `onQueue('emails')` puts email jobs on a separate queue from invoice creation ('invoices'), allowing independent scaling and preventing email failures from affecting invoice processing.
  </action>

    6. Grep confirms: `use App\Jobs\SendInvoiceEmailsJob;` import exists
    7. Verify the dispatch is AFTER the `DB::transaction` closure (not inside it) by checking line numbers
  </verify>
  <done>
